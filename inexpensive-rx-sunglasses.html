Factors Influencing Prescription Glasses Cost: Frames, Lenses, and Add-ons

The primary components determining the prescription glasses cost are the frames, lenses, and any additional coatings or treatments. Frames can range from basic plastic to premium metals, influencing both durability and style. For instance, durable budget eyeglass frames made from robust materials like TR90 often provide excellent longevity without a hefty price tag. The choice of frame also impacts comfort, with lightweight designs often being preferred.

Lenses are the heart of your eyewear, and their cost varies based on your prescription needs and the material chosen. Common options include CR-39 plastic, polycarbonate (known for impact resistance), and high-index lenses (thinner for stronger prescriptions). Choosing prescription lens materials carefully can significantly affect the overall price. Beyond the basic lens, add-ons like anti-reflective coatings, blue light filters, photochromic (light-adjusting) properties, and scratch-resistant layers further customize your experience and contribute to the final cost.
