#### **Poor Quality Materials and Visual Discomfort from Bad Lenses**

Demo lenses are typically manufactured from low-quality plastics, such as PMMA (polymethyl methacrylate). These materials are chosen for their low cost and ease of molding, not for optical clarity or durability. They are absolutely unsuitable for sustained vision correction. Unlike proper ophthalmic lenses, demo lenses are prone to causing visual aberrations, which means they can distort your vision and make objects appear wavy or blurry.

Furthermore, these lenses frequently exhibit surface defects like scratches, smudges, or inconsistencies in transparency, even when new. Such imperfections can significantly impede clear vision, leading to eye strain. Crucially, demo lenses lack essential protections vital for eye health. They offer minimal to no UV protection in eyeglasses, exposing your eyes to harmful ultraviolet radiation. They also do not include blue light filter lenses for computer use, which is increasingly important in our digital world.

The absence of an anti-reflective coating on glasses is another major drawback. Without this coating, demo lenses are highly susceptible to glare from light sources, reflections on the lens surface, and internal reflections, further reducing visual comfort and clarity. Long-term use of these inadequate lenses can result in severe visual discomfort from bad lenses. Common symptoms include persistent headaches, burning sensations in the eyes, and chronic eye fatigue. Obtaining Your Current Prescription and Pupillary Distance for Online Glasses

Before you can confidently **procure eyeglasses online**, you need your most up-to-date prescription.
